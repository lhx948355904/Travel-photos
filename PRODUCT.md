# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The product serves two primary roles:

- Admin author: the owner of the travel archive, usually returning from a trip and organizing photos by place, date, and memory.
- Visitor viewer: friends, family, or public readers who browse a map, open photo locations, and experience the trip spatially.

The main usage context is image-rich browsing on desktop and mobile, with a secondary admin workflow for adding locations, uploading photos, and editing existing memories.

## Product Purpose

Travel Photo Map is a personal travel photography map. It turns a normal album into a spatial story: every visited place appears as a photo marker on an interactive map, and each marker opens into a full-screen gallery for that location.

Success means the user can understand the travel footprint at a glance, move through places naturally, and open photos without losing the sense of geography. For the admin, success means the upload path is clear, safe, and fast enough to maintain after each trip.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, cinematic, precise.

The interface should feel like a refined photography workspace rather than a social media feed. It should give images and geography room to breathe, but still behave like a dependable tool.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, decorative card grids, purple-blue gradient hero pages, travel-blog postcard cliches, heavy glassmorphism, and interfaces where the map feels like a small widget instead of the core canvas.

Avoid over-styled controls that make standard workflows unclear. Search, login, upload, gallery navigation, and destructive actions should remain familiar and predictable.

## Design Principles

1. Map first: the map is the main interaction surface, not a background decoration.
2. Photos carry emotion: the UI should frame images cleanly and avoid visual noise around them.
3. Admin actions stay contextual: upload and edit controls appear where they help, without distracting visitors.
4. Spatial memory over raw lists: stats and panels should help users understand places, photos, and chronology.
5. Premium means reliable: responsive layout, contrast, loading states, and destructive action clarity matter more than ornamental effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard access for login, gallery close, gallery previous/next, and form submission. Respect reduced-motion preferences by disabling or simplifying decorative background motion and large transitions. Do not rely on color alone for admin state, selected photos, upload errors, or destructive actions.
